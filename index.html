A Life in Chapters

Her Story

Cuba

Childhood

Born in Cuba, Tamara grew up watching her father cook with joy — hosting friends and family around a generous table. Her mother instilled in her a love for beautiful table settings, fine plates, and the art of presentation. These early memories planted the seeds of a lifelong passion.

Angola

Age 19 — Late 1980s

At nineteen, Tamara moved to Angola as a nurse, serving during the Civil War through the late 1980s and early 1990s. In the midst of hardship, she taught herself Portuguese — reading newspapers, listening to music, and rewriting articles word by word. Her resilience became her signature.

Rio de Janeiro

1999 — Present

In 1999, Tamara arrived in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, where she rebuilt her life with the same determination that carried her through war. She embraced this new chapter with open arms, ready to turn her passion for food and hosting into something extraordinary.

The Art of Gathering

At The Table

For Tamara, the table is more than where meals are shared — it is where love is expressed, beauty is created, and memories are made. Her passion for tablescapes, inherited from her mother, transforms every gathering into art.

Passion Meets Mastery

Cooking as Art

After rebuilding her life in Rio de Janeiro, Tamara chose to follow her deepest passion. She pursued a formal education in gastronomy — not for career advancement, but purely out of love. For her, cooking has always been the most beautiful way to express care, creativity, and connection.
